The Stroop test:

There are 2 extra versions available here - one where the buttons are coloured but unlabelled and another which measures the 'velocity' of the mouse movement

Colored buttons that lack text - so just colour-matching, not reading

Test 1: Baseline test with unlabelled but coloured buttons

do the baseline test first, to get a baseline time for how long it takes you to click the buttons.
choose how many words to be displayed one at a time (from 5 to 30).
the word displayed will be the same colour as itself eg "RED" will be red.
click the button that is the same colour as the word displayed.

Test 2: True Stroop test with unlabelled but coloured buttons

choose how many words to be displayed one at a time (from 5 to 30)
the word displayed will be a different colour to itself eg "RED" will be coloured blue, or green, or yellow, but never red.
click the button that is the same COLOUR as the COLOUR of the word displayed, NOT to the colour of the WORD displayed.
compare your new time to the baseline time you got in test 1 - did it take you longer?

In this version no 'reading' is required, just colour matching, so does that make it easier with less chance of mental 'conflict'?



7 coloured buttons - with 'velocity' being measured to balance out buttons that are further from each other adding to the total time to click the buttons

Test 1: Baseline test with 7 coloured buttons

do the baseline test first, to get a baseline time for how long it takes you to click the buttons, and a baseline 'velocity' (velocity = pixels moved / time taken) so you are not penalised for having to move from 'violet' across to 'red', compared to moving say from 'green' to 'cyan' or other adjacent colours.
choose how many words to be displayed one at a time (from 5 to 30).
the word displayed will be the same colour as itself eg "RED" will be red.
click the button that corresponds to the colour and the word displayed.

Test 2: True Stroop test with 7 coloured buttons

choose how many words to be displayed one at a time (from 5 to 30)
the word displayed will be a different colour to itself eg "RED" will be coloured any of 6 other colours, but never red.
click the button that corresponds to the COLOUR of the word displayed, NOT to the word displayed.
compare your new time to the baseline time you got in test 1 - did it take you longer?
