Basic instructions for using the eMac Nucleus Constructor Kit

MOVING NUCLEONS

Drag the nucleons from where they are stored in the black box into the boxed viewing area.

To select a nucleon click on any part of it.

Drag the nucleon with the mouse to change its 'x' or 'y' position.

To change a nucleon's 'z' position you use the keyboard:
Z pushes the last selected nucleon further down into the page.
Q pulls the last selected nucleon up out of the page.
Each nucleon displays its 'z' coordinate, so you can 'see' where exactly they are. The displayed number refers to the centre spot of the nucleon. The nucleons have a radius of 25 units so their edges are 25 units greater or lesser than the displayed values.

Nucleons can 'feel' each other and cannot occupy the same space so their movement left/right, up/down, in/out will be impeded if there is another nucleon in the way

FOCUS

The last selected nucleon has the FOCUS on it.
It's x and y position are displayed at the top, and the number of other nucleons that it is touching. When you use the Z, Q, F keys these all act on the nucleon that has the focus.
You can change the focus to a new nucleon by just clicking on it.
Pressing S highlights (Shows) which nucleon currently has the focus (pressing S again turns off the highlight). Pressing J Jumps the focus to the next nucleon in sequence (useful if the nucleon you want it buried under lots of others and you cannot get to it with the mouse).

ROTATING THE VIEW POINT

The cursor keys will rotate whatever is inside the viewing box (around the 2 axes shown - these axes are at z=250)).
The 'left' cursor rotates the nucleus around to the left etc.

(if your cursor keys don't work - which might happen in Netscape then...
use N and M to rotate left/right
and O and L to rotate up/down)
